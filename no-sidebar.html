A Love For Nature

“Keep your love of nature, for that is the true way to understand art more and more.” ~Vincent Van Gogh

Nature's influence

Nature has captivated me ever since I first laid eyes on the Yosemite Valley at the young age of 6. From the towering wall of El Capitain and the perfectly sliced Half Dome to the powerful endless outpour of the Yosemite Falls. The awe, excitment and wonder I felt all those years ago are the same emotions I get today each time I am immersed in nature.

This love for the outdoors is what inspired me to buy a camera. We all have a unique connection to nature and that gives each one of us a unique perspective. Some can find beauty in a patch of fallen leaves on the forest floor while others notice the mesmorizing symmetry shown through puddle. Photography is a way to share my perspective and to inspire others to spend more time in nature and have their own little adventures.

Mountains or beach?

This is a question I have been asked several times and seems common around nature enthusiasts. Only being allowed to choose one, my answer is mountains. There is a stronger call of adventure in mountains to me.

Mt. Fuji

I took this picture while hiking in the Yamanashi prefecture. The sacred and iconic symbol of Japan stands at 12,388ft (3,777 metres), making it the highest peak in Japan and can be seen from several surrounding prefectures.

The Great Wall of China

This photo was taken by me at Huanghuacheng, "Yellow Flower City" and is the only lakeside Great Wall in Beijing. Being one of the 7 world wonders The Great Wall is a symbol of enduring strength. There are many sections of the wall that are not preserved and are thus known as the "Wild Wall."

Kolukkumalai

I took this picture during my trip to Kerala, India. At an altitude of 7,900 feet, Kolukkumalai tea plantations are the highest in the world. This high altitude gives the tea a special flavor and freshness you won't find anywhere else in the world.
